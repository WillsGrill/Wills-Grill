Smoky Turkey, Mushroom & Butter Bean Casserole

With Red Peppers, Spinach & Lemon

Ingredients

  • For the casserole

  • 1000 g Turkey Mince
  • 2 each White Onion
  • 400 g Mushrooms
  • 3 each Red Pepper
  • 480 g Butter Beans
  • 800 g Chopped Tomatoes
  • 400 ml Low-Salt Chicken Stock
  • 4 tsp Easy Garlic
  • 4 tsp Smoked Paprika
  • 2 tsp Ground Cumin
  • 2 tsp Dried Thyme
  • 2 tbsp Olive Oil

  • To finish

  • 240 g Baby Spinach
  • 2 tbsp Bottled Lemon Juice

Method

  1. 1
    Heat half the olive oil in a large lidded casserole over a medium-high heat and brown the turkey mince in two batches, transferring each batch to a clean plate.
  2. 2
    Add the remaining olive oil, diced onions and diced red peppers to the casserole and cook for six minutes until softened.
  3. 3
    Add the sliced mushrooms and cook over a medium-high heat until their moisture evaporates and they begin to brown.
  4. 4
    Stir in the Easy Garlic, smoked paprika, ground cumin and dried thyme for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. 5
    Return the turkey to the casserole, pour in the chopped tomatoes and low-salt chicken stock and stir thoroughly.
  6. 6
    Cover and simmer gently for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the turkey is completely cooked with no pink remaining.
  7. 7
    Stir in the drained butter beans and simmer uncovered for eight minutes until the casserole has thickened.
  8. 8
    Fold through the baby spinach and bottled lemon juice, allow the spinach to wilt, then season with pepper and salt only if needed.
