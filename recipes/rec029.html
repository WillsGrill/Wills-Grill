Healthy Pork Satay Bowls

With Brown Rice, Crunchy Slaw & Quick-Pickled Cucumber

Ingredients

  • 300 g Pork Tenderloin
  • 120 g Brown Rice
  • 0.5 each Cucumber
  • 120 g Red Cabbage
  • 100 g Carrots
  • 2 each Spring Onions
  • 40 g Natural Peanut Butter
  • 2 tbsp Low-Salt Soy Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Rice Vinegar
  • 1 each Lime
  • 1 tbsp Honey
  • 10 g Fresh Ginger
  • 1 clove Garlic
  • 1 tbsp Rapeseed Oil

Method

  1. 1
    Cook the brown rice according to the packet instructions, then drain and keep warm.
  2. 2
    Use a vegetable peeler to make cucumber ribbons, then toss them with one tablespoon of rice vinegar and half the honey and set aside to pickle.
  3. 3
    Finely shred the red cabbage, grate the carrot and slice the spring onions, then toss together with half the lime juice.
  4. 4
    Whisk the peanut butter, low-salt soy sauce, remaining rice vinegar and honey, grated ginger, crushed garlic, remaining lime juice and four tablespoons of warm water until smooth.
  5. 5
    Trim the pork tenderloin, slice thinly and coat with one-third of the satay sauce.
  6. 6
    Heat the rapeseed oil in a non-stick frying pan and cook the pork for five to seven minutes, turning, until browned and cooked through.
  7. 7
    Add the remaining satay sauce to the pan and toss for one minute until hot and lightly thickened.
  8. 8
    Build the bowls with brown rice, crunchy slaw, drained pickled cucumber and pork satay, then scatter over the spring onions.
