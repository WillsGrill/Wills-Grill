BBQ Sirloin Steak with Chimichurri

With Charred Pak Choi & Sweet Potato Fries

Ingredients

  • 300 g Lean Sirloin Steak
  • 400 g Sweet Potatoes
  • 2 each Pak Choi
  • 20 g Fresh Parsley
  • 15 g Fresh Coriander
  • 1 tsp Easy Garlic
  • 1 tbsp Red Wine Vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 1 tsp Smoked Paprika
  • 0.25 tsp Chilli Flakes
  • 0.5 tsp Black Pepper
  • 0.25 tsp Sea Salt

Method

  1. 1
    Heat the oven to 220°C/200°C fan and line a large tray with baking paper.
  2. 2
    Cut the sweet potatoes into slim fries, toss with one tablespoon of olive oil, smoked paprika and black pepper, then roast for 25 to 30 minutes, turning halfway.
  3. 3
    Finely chop the parsley and coriander and mix with the Easy Garlic, red wine vinegar, chilli flakes, one tablespoon of olive oil and a tablespoon of water to make the chimichurri.
  4. 4
    Trim any thick outer fat from the sirloin, pat dry and season lightly with salt and black pepper.
  5. 5
    Cook the steak on a very hot barbecue or griddle for two to four minutes per side, depending on thickness and how you like it cooked.
  6. 6
    Transfer the steak to a board, spoon over a little chimichurri and rest for five to eight minutes.
  7. 7
    Halve the pak choi lengthways and char it cut-side down for two to three minutes, then turn and cook until just tender.
  8. 8
    Slice the steak across the grain and serve with the pak choi, sweet potato fries and remaining chimichurri.
