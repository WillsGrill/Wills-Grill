Venison Steak with Blackberry Sauce

With Roasted Carrots, Parsnips & Sweet Potato

Ingredients

  • 300 g Venison Steak
  • 200 g Carrots
  • 200 g Parsnips
  • 300 g Sweet Potatoes
  • 120 g Blackberries
  • 1 each Shallots
  • 0.5 cube Beef Stock
  • 1 tbsp Balsamic Vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 5 g Fresh Rosemary
  • 0.5 tsp Black Pepper

Method

  1. 1
    Heat the oven to 220°C/200°C fan and place a large baking tray inside to heat.
  2. 2
    Cut the carrots, parsnips and sweet potato into slim, even batons and toss with half the olive oil, rosemary and black pepper.
  3. 3
    Spread the vegetables on the hot tray and roast for 25 to 30 minutes, turning once, until tender and caramelised.
  4. 4
    Pat the venison dry, season with black pepper and rub with the remaining olive oil.
  5. 5
    Sear the steaks in a very hot frying pan for two to three minutes per side for medium-rare, or to your liking, then rest loosely covered.
  6. 6
    Lower the heat, add the finely chopped shallot to the same pan and cook for two minutes.
  7. 7
    Add the blackberries, balsamic vinegar, half a beef stock cube and 150ml water; simmer for five minutes, lightly crushing some berries until the sauce is glossy.
  8. 8
    Slice the rested venison across the grain and serve with the roasted roots and warm blackberry sauce.
